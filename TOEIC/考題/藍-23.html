解題關鍵

Q2
The health authority has issued a directive that all licensed practitioners ＿＿ annual continuing education credits.
【中譯】衛生主管機關已發布指令，要求所有領有執照的從業人員__年度繼續教育學分。
A

B

C

D

解題關鍵
directive that + S + V（原形）= 官方指令要求某事，此為 subjunctive mood（假設語氣/命令語氣）中的原形動詞用法。mandate 在此作動詞（強制規定），符合虛擬語氣結構。
陷阱提示
mandate 既是名詞也是動詞，考生可能誤以為此處需要被動語態；to be mandated 雖含被動意味，但語法上在 that 子句中不合結構。
文法補充
名詞子句命令語氣（Subjunctive after directive/order/requirement that + S + V原形）+ mandate 的動詞用法

to be mandatedv-phr/tuː biː ˈmændeɪtɪd/被強制規定（被動不定詞）被動不定詞需主詞，此處子句主詞已為 all practitioners，語法不整
will have mandatedv-phr/wɪl hæv ˈmændeɪtɪd/將已完成強制規定（未來完成式）時態與指令發布情境不符

解題關鍵

Q9
The success of the renovation project was attributed not so much to the generous budget ＿＿ to the exceptional coordination between the design team and the construction crew.
【中譯】翻新專案的成功與其說是歸功於充裕的預算，不如說是歸功於設計團隊與施工隊之間出色的協調。
A
as
B
but
C
than
D
rather
解題關鍵
not so much A as B = 「與其說A不如說B」，為正式書面固定搭配；強調兩者都有貢獻但B更為關鍵，語氣比 not A but B 更委婉
陷阱提示
not...but 語氣更強烈（完全否定A）；not...than 搭配比較級（more than / rather than）；not...rather 非固定搭配
文法補充
not so much A as B 辨析：① 表「程度上B比A更貼切」，帶有「A也有一定作用，但B更主要」的細微差別；② 比 not A but B（直接否定A）更委婉正式；③ 常見句型：'The challenge is not so much finding data as interpreting it'（挑戰與其說是找資料，不如說是詮釋資料）
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
asprep./conj./æz/如；相當於（not so much A as B 的固定搭配）not so much A as B = 與其說是A，不如說是B ✓（強調B比A更貼切描述現實）
butconj./bʌt/但是；而是not A but B = 不是A而是B（直接替換，比 not so much...as 語氣更強、更絕對，否定A的程度不同）
thanconj./ðæn/比（比較級連接詞）more A than B / rather than（比較結構；not so much 不與 than 搭配）✗
ratheradv./conj./ˈræðər/相當；寧可；更確切地說rather than + N/V-ing（對比偏好；not so much 的固定搭配為 as，非 rather）✗

解題關鍵

文法補充
By the time + 現在式子句, 主句 + will have + p.p.（未來完成式）

科技新創：量子運算技術在企業資料安全上的應用前景
TOEIC · article
Quantum Computing and the Future of Corporate Data Security

In the wake of several high-profile data breaches affecting Fortune 500 companies, cybersecurity experts are increasingly turning their attention to quantum computing as both a potential threat and a transformative solution. Unlike conventional computers, which process information in binary form, quantum computers leverage the principles of superposition and entanglement to perform complex calculations at unprecedented speeds. While this capability offers exciting prospects for industries ranging from pharmaceuticals to logistics, it also poses a formidable challenge to current encryption standards.

Most widely used encryption protocols, including RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography, rely on the computational difficulty of factoring large prime numbers. Quantum computers, once sufficiently advanced, could theoretically solve these problems in a fraction of the time required by classical machines, rendering existing security infrastructure obsolete. This prospect—sometimes referred to as the "quantum threat"—has prompted regulatory bodies and technology firms alike to begin investing in post-quantum cryptography, a field dedicated to developing encryption methods resistant to quantum attacks.

Despite the urgency, adoption of quantum-resistant security measures remains slow across many industries. Experts caution that organizations which delay updating their encryption standards risk becoming vulnerable well before quantum computers reach commercial viability. The window for proactive transition, they warn, is narrowing.
