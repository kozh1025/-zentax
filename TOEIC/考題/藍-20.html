Q1
Under the new data governance policy, all departments are required to ＿＿ employee records for a minimum of five years.
【中譯】根據新的資料治理政策，所有部門須將員工記錄__至少五年。
A
retain
B
retrieve
C
discard
D
redact
解題關鍵
retain records/files/data = 保留/留存記錄，是法規合規類文件的核心搭配動詞；minimum of five years（至少五年）是 retain 的典型時間框架語境。
陷阱提示
retrieve 意為「取回/提取」，指主動拿出已存的資料，不表長期保存；redact 是將文件中的敏感資訊遮蔽，語義完全不同。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
retainv./rɪˈteɪn/保留；留存；持有retain records / retain staff ✓
retrievev./rɪˈtriːv/取回；提取retrieve data（主動提取，非長期保存）
discardv./dɪˈskɑːrd/丟棄；捨棄discard old files（語義相反）
redactv./rɪˈdækt/編輯；刪去（機密內容）redact a report（法律文件編輯，非保存）
字詞家族

v. 保留；留用

n. 保留；留存率

Sustainable Hotel Certification Programs: Trends and Outcomes

The proliferation of sustainable hotel certification programs over the past decade has fundamentally altered the competitive landscape of the hospitality industry. According to data compiled by the Global Hospitality Research Institute (GHRI), properties bearing internationally recognized green certifications have reported, on average, a 12% premium in average daily rate (ADR) compared to non-certified counterparts in the same market segment.

Despite these financial incentives, adoption rates among independent operators remain considerably lower than those recorded by international chain affiliates. A 2025 survey of over 3,000 hotel operators across 40 countries found that 68% of independent properties cited the initial compliance costs—which can range from $15,000 to over $80,000 depending on property size—as the primary barrier to certification. Chain-affiliated hotels, by contrast, benefit from centralized sustainability infrastructure and shared corporate resources, enabling a more streamlined path to compliance.

Beyond direct financial returns, certified properties have demonstrated measurable improvements in guest satisfaction scores. The GHRI report notes that certified hotels averaged a 0.4-point increase on a 5-point guest satisfaction scale, a margin that hospitality analysts describe as statistically significant given the scale of the industry. Furthermore, employee retention rates at certified properties were found to be 9% higher on average, suggesting that sustainability credentials may also serve as an effective talent attraction and retention tool.

Looking ahead, the report projects continued growth in certification uptake, driven largely by evolving traveler preferences among younger demographics and mounting pressure from corporate travel procurement policies that increasingly mandate green-certified accommodations.

Embracing Neurodiversity in the Workplace

A growing number of corporations are recognizing that neurodiversity — the natural variation in human brain function and behavioral traits — represents an untapped source of talent. Conditions such as autism spectrum disorder, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, and dyslexia are increasingly being viewed not as limitations, but as cognitive differences that can bring distinct advantages in specific professional contexts. Companies in the technology, finance, and creative industries have been among the earliest adopters of formal neurodiversity hiring and inclusion programs.

The business case for neurodiversity initiatives is compelling. Research indicates that neurodiverse employees often excel in tasks requiring pattern recognition, systematic thinking, and sustained concentration. Some organizations report measurable gains in innovation and problem-solving quality after implementing dedicated support structures such as flexible workspaces, adjusted communication formats, and mentorship programs tailored to individual needs. These accommodations, contrary to some initial concerns, have generally been found to benefit the broader workforce rather than create divisions.

Despite progress, significant challenges remain. Many managers lack the training necessary to support neurodiverse team members effectively, and workplace norms continue to favor conventional communication and behavior patterns. Advocates argue that lasting change requires not only structural accommodations but also a fundamental shift in organizational culture — one that values diverse cognitive styles as strategic assets rather than deviations from the norm.
