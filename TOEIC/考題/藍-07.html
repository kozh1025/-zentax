Q1
＿＿ notified of the security breach, the IT team immediately launched a full system audit.
【中譯】__接獲安全漏洞通知後，資訊科技團隊立即啟動完整系統稽核。
A
Having been
B
Being
C
After being
D
To be
解題關鍵
IT team 是被通知的對象（被動），且通知動作先於稽核（先行）→ 完成被動分詞 Having been notified；Being notified 只表同時；After being 保留連接詞不省略；To be 表目的不表先行完成。
陷阱提示
Being notified（同時）vs. Having been notified（先行）：稽核在通知之後發生，需強調時序先後 → Having been。After being 雖語義正確但不構成省略連接詞的分詞構句形式。
文法補充
Having been + p.p.（完成被動分詞構句）= After the subject had been + p.p.；主句主語必須是被動動作的接受者
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
Having beenph/ˈhævɪŋ biːn/完成被動分詞（先行被動）Having been + p.p. → 主語先被動 ✓
Beingv-ing/ˈbiːɪŋ/現在被動分詞（同時被動）Being + p.p. → 動作與主句同時
After beingph/ˈæftər ˈbiːɪŋ/在被...後（保留介詞 after）明確標示時序但不省略 after
To beph/tuː biː/不定詞被動（目的/未來）To be + p.p. → 表目的或未來

Q3
Although the finance department submitted their compliance report last week, the legal team ＿＿ review it in full and has requested a two-day extension.
【中譯】雖然財務部門上週已提交合規報告，法務團隊__全面審閱，並已申請延長兩天。
A
has yet to
B
has been to
C
has failed to
D
has not to
解題關鍵
have yet to + V = 「尚未做某事，但預期將完成」；為正式書面用語，帶有任務尚未完成的中性語氣，不含負面評判，常用於進度報告情境
陷阱提示
has been to 表去過某地的經歷；has failed to 帶失敗/過失的負面語氣（語意過強）；has not to 為非標準語法
文法補充
have yet to + V 辨析：① 語意：尚未（預期完成），非「不需要」也非「無法」② 形式：have/has yet to + V 原形③ 中性語氣：比 have failed to（含負面評判）更適合描述進行中工作的未完成狀態④ 常見語境：We have yet to receive confirmation = 我們尚未收到確認
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
has yet tophr./hæz jɛt tuː/尚未…（表任務未完成，預期將完成）have yet to + V = 尚未做某事（正式書面，帶有「預期即將完成」的中性語氣）✓
has been tophr./hæz biːn tuː/曾去過某地（表過去經歷）have been to + place = 曾去過某地（表達去過某地的經驗，與「尚未完成任務」語意完全不同）✗
has failed tophr./hæz feɪld tuː/未能…（帶有失敗或過失的負面含義）have failed to + V = 無法/未能（語氣較負面，暗示責任疏失；此句僅說「尚未完成，已申請延期」，非過失）✗
has not tophr.（非標準英語結構）have not to + V（✗）：非標準語法；表示「沒義務」應用 does not have to，have not to 為錯誤形式

Q7
The vendor agreement will remain valid ＿＿ all deliveries are made within the agreed timeframes.
【中譯】只要所有交貨均在約定時間範圍內完成，供應商協議將持續有效。
A

B
so long as
C

D

解題關鍵
so long as 與 as long as 同義，均表「只要……就」的條件；provided that 亦可表條件但語氣偏向法律合約前提。此題語境為「持續有效的條件」，so long as 最為自然，且為本輪目標文法。in case 表「以防萬一」，語意不符。
陷阱提示
so long as / provided that 兩者雖均可表條件，但 provided that 暗示「前提假設」，而 so long as 強調持續性條件，此處語意更接近持續有效
文法補充
條件副詞子句連接詞：so long as = as long as（持續條件）vs provided that（法律前提條件）
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法

ESG Investing: From Niche to Mainstream

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria have rapidly moved from a niche concern among ethical investors to a mainstream consideration for institutional fund managers worldwide. According to a recent industry report, assets managed under ESG-aligned strategies surpassed $35 trillion globally in 2025, representing nearly 40 percent of total assets under management.

Proponents argue that companies demonstrating strong ESG performance are better positioned to manage long-term risks, including regulatory changes, resource scarcity, and reputational damage. A study conducted by a leading consultancy found that firms with high ESG scores achieved an average return on equity 2.3 percentage points higher than industry peers over a five-year period.

Nevertheless, the field is not without controversy. Detractors have raised concerns about "greenwashing," whereby companies disclose selectively favorable ESG data while concealing underperforming metrics. Regulatory bodies in multiple jurisdictions have responded by introducing mandatory disclosure frameworks. The European Union's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), which took effect in January 2024, requires large companies to report on a broad range of sustainability indicators using standardized methodologies.

For investors, the challenge lies in distinguishing genuine ESG leaders from those that merely perform well on self-reported indices. Third-party rating agencies have emerged to address this gap, though critics note that these agencies often disagree significantly on scores for the same company, raising questions about the reliability of ESG metrics as a whole.

Digital Subscriptions Reshape Academic Publishing Landscape

Universities around the world are rapidly transitioning from print journal subscriptions to digital access models, driven by cost pressures, space limitations, and growing demand for remote access. In view of these shifts, academic publishers have been compelled to redesign their pricing structures and content delivery systems.

The report also highlighted significant disparities in subscription costs between large research universities and smaller liberal arts colleges, with the latter often facing disproportionately higher per-article fees.

Critics have long raised concerns about the dominance of a handful of major publishers who control the pricing of thousands of journals. Several universities have responded by withdrawing from expensive bundle agreements and investing in open-access publishing initiatives instead. Proponents argue that open-access models democratize knowledge by making research freely available to institutions that cannot afford premium subscriptions.

Despite the growing momentum of open access, commercial publishers maintain that subscription revenues are necessary to fund peer-review processes and maintain editorial quality. The debate over how to balance financial sustainability with broad knowledge accessibility continues to shape academic publishing policy worldwide.
