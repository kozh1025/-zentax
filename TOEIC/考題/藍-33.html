Q4
Ms. Harper will be ＿＿ the overseas expansion project while the director is on leave next month.
【中譯】下個月主管請假期間，Harper女士將負責監管海外擴張專案。
A
in charge of
B
responsible for
C
accountable to
D
in lieu of
解題關鍵
空格後接 the overseas expansion project（受詞為事物），且語境強調 Ms. Harper「主導管理」該專案。in charge of 表示「負責管理某事或某項目」，後接事物，語意最為貼切。responsible for 雖同樣後接事物，但偏向「對結果承擔責任」，語氣較消極；in charge of 強調「有指揮權」。accountable to 後接「人」（向某人匯報）；in lieu of 意為「代替」，語意不符。
陷阱提示
in charge of 與 responsible for 的語意非常接近，是高頻混淆點：in charge of 強調「有主導權、指揮權」；responsible for 強調「對某結果或行為承擔責任」。兩者後接受詞皆為事物，但語用情境有差異。
文法補充
介系詞片語搭配規則：in charge of + 事物（負責管理）；responsible for + 事物/行為（對…負責）；accountable to + 人（向…負責/交代）；in lieu of + 名詞（取代）。注意 accountable to 後接人而非事物，這是區辨關鍵。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
in charge ofprep. phr./ɪn tʃɑːrdʒ əv/負責；主管be in charge of + 事物（負責管理某事）
responsible forprep. phr./rɪˈspɒnsɪbl fɔːr/對…負責be responsible for + 事物/行為（承擔責任）
accountable toprep. phr./əˈkaʊntəbl tuː/向…負責；向…交代be accountable to + 人（向某人匯報交代）
in lieu ofprep. phr./ɪn luː əv/代替；取代in lieu of + 名詞（作為…的替代）

The airline's new loyalty program remains somewhat ＿＿ , with several key features still under development and the full rollout not expected until the second quarter.

陷阱提示
nascent 與 inchoate 均形容「早期」，但 nascent 聚焦「剛開始萌生」，inchoate 聚焦「已啟動但尚未完整成形/有序」，兩者語意細微差異是 C1 陷阱。

Why Loyalty Programs Have Become a Retail Imperative

In an era of intense competition and shrinking brand loyalty, retailers across sectors are turning to structured loyalty programs as a primary tool for customer retention. Whereas simple punch cards and occasional coupons once sufficed, today's consumers expect seamless, personalized rewards experiences delivered through digital channels.

The most effective modern loyalty programs share several characteristics. They offer tiered membership structures that incentivize progressive spending, personalized offers based on purchase history, and real-time reward tracking through dedicated mobile applications. Retailers that have invested in these capabilities report measurably higher repeat purchase rates and increased average transaction values among enrolled members compared to non-members.

However, designing a successful loyalty program is far from straightforward. One of the most common pitfalls is overpromising rewards that the business cannot sustainably deliver. Programs that reduce point values or restrict redemption options after launch tend to generate significant customer backlash and reputational damage. Transparency in program terms from the outset is therefore critical.

Data privacy also presents a growing challenge. Loyalty programs depend on the collection and analysis of detailed consumer behavior data, raising regulatory scrutiny in markets with robust privacy legislation. Retailers must strike a careful balance between personalization and compliance, ensuring that data collection practices align with applicable legal frameworks.

Despite these challenges, the strategic value of a well-executed loyalty program remains compelling. Analysts estimate that loyal customers spend on average between twenty and thirty percent more annually than occasional shoppers, making customer retention one of the highest-return investments in retail.

法律合規：跨境資料移轉新規的企業因應策略
TOEIC · report
A new wave of cross-border data transfer regulations is reshaping how multinational companies structure their data governance frameworks. Building on the foundations established by the European General Data Protection Regulation, regulators in multiple jurisdictions have introduced stricter requirements for documenting the legal basis under which personal data may be transferred across national boundaries. Unlike earlier frameworks that relied primarily on standard contractual clauses, the updated rules require companies to conduct transfer impact assessments that evaluate the adequacy of data protection standards in the recipient country.

For many organizations, the compliance burden is substantial. Legal teams must now review and update data processing agreements with every third-party vendor that handles personal data on behalf of the company. Failure to maintain current documentation can expose organizations to regulatory fines that, in some jurisdictions, can reach up to four percent of global annual revenue. Industry analysts note that smaller enterprises, which typically lack dedicated legal resources, face disproportionate compliance costs relative to their larger counterparts.

Despite these challenges, a number of technology vendors have responded by offering automated compliance platforms that help organizations map their data flows, generate impact assessments, and monitor regulatory updates in real time. These tools cannot replace legal judgment, but they can significantly reduce the administrative overhead associated with maintaining compliance across multiple regulatory regimes. Organizations that invest early in such infrastructure are better positioned to adapt as the regulatory landscape continues to evolve.
