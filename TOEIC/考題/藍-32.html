Q4
Little ＿＿ the regional director realize, when approving the aggressive store expansion plan, that shifting consumer preferences would leave several of the new locations struggling to achieve profitability within their first year.
【中譯】地區總監在批准積極的門市擴張計畫時，__沒有意識到消費者偏好的轉變將使幾個新門市在第一年內難以實現盈利。
A
did
B
does
C
the director did
D
was
解題關鍵
Little + did + S + V原形 = 否定副詞前置倒裝；Little 表「幾乎不/未曾意識到」，過去語境用 did，述語為一般動詞（realize）用 did 而非 was
陷阱提示
does 時態錯誤（現在式 vs. 過去式）；was 述語錯誤（be 動詞不接原形）；正常語序違反否定前置倒裝規則
文法補充
Little did + S + V 否定副詞倒裝辨析：① 觸發詞：Little / Rarely / Seldom / Never + aux. + S + V ② 時態選擇：過去事件 → did；現在事件 → does（單數）/do（複數）；完成式 → has/have ③ 常見搭配：Little did S know/realize/expect + that 子句 = 表「幾乎不知道/意識到/預料到」④ 正常語序還原：The regional director little realized that... ≈ The regional director had no idea that...
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
didaux./dɪd/（助動詞 do，過去式）Little + did + S + V原形 = 否定副詞前置倒裝 ✓（Little 置句首，助動詞 did 前移至主語 the director 之前；時態為過去式，後接原形動詞 realize）
doesaux./dʌz/（助動詞 do，現在式單數）Little + does + S + V原形（現在式）✗：此句語境為過去批准決策（when approving...），需過去式 did，現在式 does 時態不符
the director didphr.（主語＋助動詞，正常語序）Little + the director did（✗）：否定副詞 Little 前置後，主句必須採倒裝語序（助動詞先於主語），不可維持正常語序
wasaux./wɒz/（助動詞 be，過去式）Little + was + S + adj./p.p.（過去被動或形容詞述語）✗：此句述語為一般動詞 realize（原形），非形容詞或被動式；was 後不接原形動詞
Q5
According to the latest HR study, ＿＿ employees attend professional training, __ they tend to be in their daily tasks.
【中譯】根據最新人力資源研究，員工參加專業培訓的頻率__高，日常工作中的生產力__強。
A
the more regularly / the more productive
B
the more regularly / more productive
C
as more regularly / so more productive
D
more regularly / the more productive
解題關鍵
The + 比較級 + S+V, the + 比較級 + S+V 為固定句型，表示兩者成正比。兩項均須有 the，缺一即錯。
陷阱提示
B 缺第二個 the；C 誤用 as...so 句型；D 首項缺 The。四個選項各測一個不同的常見錯誤點。
文法補充
The + 比較級 + S+V, the + 比較級 + S+V：表示兩件事成正比變化，兩個分句均須以 the + 比較級開頭，缺少任一 the 均為語法錯誤。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
the more regularly / the more productiveThe+比較級…, the+比較級…/ðə/越規律…就越有生產力✓ 標準雙重比較級結構
the more regularly / more productive缺第二個 the/ðə/第二項缺定冠詞 the錯：the more...需對稱 the more...
as more regularly / so more productiveas...so 結構/æz/as...so 為比例用法錯：as more... 不合語法
more regularly / the more productive首項缺 The/-/第一項缺定冠詞 the錯：兩項均須以 the 開頭

TOEIC · article
Whistleblower Protections in the Corporate Sector: Progress and Persistent Gaps

Over the past decade, the legislative framework for whistleblower protection in the corporate sector has undergone significant expansion in many jurisdictions. In the United States, the Dodd-Frank Act and the Sarbanes-Oxley Act collectively establish financial incentives and legal safeguards for employees who report securities fraud or accounting irregularities to regulatory authorities. Similarly, the European Union's 2021 Whistleblowing Directive obligated member states to create accessible internal and external reporting channels by specified deadlines.

Despite these legislative advances, a persistent gap between formal protections and practical outcomes continues to be documented by researchers and advocacy groups. A 2024 study published in the Corporate Governance Quarterly found that 61% of employees who considered reporting workplace misconduct ultimately chose not to do so, citing fears of retaliation as the primary deterrent. Notably, this figure remained largely unchanged from a comparable 2019 survey, suggesting that the enactment of new legislation alone has proven insufficient to shift workplace culture.

Legal experts point to the inconsistent enforcement of existing protections as a central cause of this gap. When employers successfully contest whistleblower claims or when regulatory agencies delay action on complaints, the chilling effect on potential reporters is amplified. Some jurisdictions have responded by introducing mandatory disclosure requirements for corporations and extending statutes of limitations for retaliation claims.

The rapid expansion of cross-border e-commerce has placed unprecedented pressure on warehousing infrastructure worldwide. Retailers that once relied on manual sorting and batch-picking methods are now investing heavily in automated storage and retrieval systems (AS/RS) to meet the speed and accuracy demanded by international customers.
