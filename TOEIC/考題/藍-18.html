Q1

B

C

D

解題關鍵

Q8
Employees may access the confidential database ＿＿ they obtain written authorization from the compliance officer beforehand.
【中譯】員工可以存取機密資料庫，__ 他們事先取得合規主任的書面授權。
A
as long as
B
provided that
C

D

解題關鍵
provided that 強調「前提條件」，語氣比 as long as 更正式，常見於法律、合約文件；in case 表預防，even if 表讓步，均不符語境。
陷阱提示
as long as vs provided that：兩者均可表條件，但 provided that 語感更偏向「法律/正式書面約束」；選項中正式合規文件語境偏好 provided that。

provided that = 正式條件連接詞，可縮略為 provided；so long as 同義但略口語化；兩者後接直述語氣。

Q12

【中譯】季度盈餘報告表現__令人失望，以至於董事會召開了緊急會議。

B

解題關鍵
so + 形容詞/副詞 + that 引導結果子句；本句空格後為形容詞 disappointing（無名詞），故選 so 而非 such；too 接不定詞不接 that 子句；very 不引導結果子句。

Remote Work and the Reshaping of Urban Real Estate Markets

The widespread adoption of remote and hybrid work models has produced one of the most significant shifts in urban real estate patterns in recent history. Regardless of whether companies eventually mandate a return to the office, the structural changes already set in motion appear likely to persist for years to come.

In major metropolitan areas, commercial office vacancy rates have climbed to record highs. In some city centers, vacancies now exceed thirty percent, compared to a pre-pandemic average of under ten percent. Property owners and municipal governments alike are grappling with how to repurpose underutilized commercial space. Several cities have introduced zoning reforms to convert obsolete office towers into residential units, though the process remains expensive and technically complex.

Meanwhile, suburban and exurban residential markets have experienced robust demand growth. Buyers and renters prioritizing home office space, larger floor plans, and proximity to green spaces have driven prices in outer rings upward. This trend has contributed to affordability pressures spreading beyond the urban core into communities that were previously considered affordable.

Real estate analysts caution that the long-term implications are still unfolding. Technology companies and financial firms have adopted divergent approaches to remote work policy, making it difficult to project aggregate demand for commercial space with precision. What does appear clear, however, is that the binary view of urban versus suburban living is giving way to a more nuanced spectrum of residential preferences shaped by individual work arrangements.
