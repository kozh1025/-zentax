Q1
Employees must notify their direct manager ＿＿ they become aware of any potential compliance issues that may affect the upcoming audit.
【中譯】員工__察覺到任何可能影響即將進行之稽核的法規遵循疑慮，就必須立刻通知其直屬主管。
A
as soon as
B
as long as
C
by the time
D
in case
解題關鍵
as soon as + S + V（現在式） = 時間副詞子句，表「一…就立刻…」，強調動作之間無時間差；時間子句不用 will
陷阱提示
as long as 強調持續條件而非即時性；by the time 強調截止點；in case 強調預防性，三者均不能表達「一察覺就立刻通知」的即時義
文法補充
時間副詞子句（as soon as / when / after / before / by the time）不使用未來式 will，一律用現在式代替未來時態：as soon as they become aware（✓）vs as soon as they will become aware（✗）
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
as soon asconj./æz suːn æz/一…就；立刻當…時（時間副詞子句）as soon as + S + V（現在式）= 一旦…就立即…，強調時間的即時性 ✓
as long asconj./æz lɒŋ æz/只要（條件持續）as long as + S + V = 只要…就…（偏持續條件，非「一發生就立刻」的即時性）
by the timeconj./baɪ ðə taɪm/等到…時（截止時間點）by the time + S + V = 等到某事發生時（強調截止點，語意偏「到時候為止」，非即時通知）
in caseconj./ɪn keɪs/以防萬一（預防性條件）in case + S + V = 預先做準備以防問題發生（預防性，非「問題已發生時立刻通報」）

Q4

【中譯】在公司退休金計畫中__超過十年，哈靈頓先生現在有資格提早退休。
A
Enrolled
B
Having enrolled
C

D

解題關鍵
enroll in 為「主動加入」，主語（Mr. Harrington）是執行者，故用主動完成分詞 Having enrolled。若用 Having been enrolled 則語意轉為被動（被登記），一般情境較少見。Having enrolled 最自然。
陷阱提示
Enrolled 開頭（過去分詞）常讓人誤以為分詞構句正確，但過去分詞構句表被動，需確認主被動關係。To have enrolled 是不定詞完成式，用法不同於分詞構句。
文法補充
完成分詞構句：Having + p.p.（主動） / Having been + p.p.（被動），表示先於主句發生的動作。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
Enrolledv(p.p.)/ɪnˈroʊld/已登記（被動分詞構句）enrolled 為過去分詞，語意被動，但本題主語主動參與 ✗
Having enrolledv phr/ˈhævɪŋ ɪnˈroʊld/（主動）完成登記後（完成分詞）強調動作完成後的狀態，語意完整 ✓
Having been enrolledv phr/ˈhævɪŋ biːn ɪnˈroʊld/（被動）完成被登記被動完成分詞；enroll 可及物亦可不及物，需確認主被動

TOEIC · article
The rise of eco-certification programs in the hospitality industry has fundamentally altered how travelers select accommodations. A recent industry survey found that more than sixty percent of leisure travelers under forty now actively seek hotels holding recognized green certifications, such as the Global Sustainable Tourism Council (GSTC) standard or regional equivalents. The appeal of certified properties extends beyond environmental idealism; certified hotels often report lower utility costs, which can translate into more competitive room rates.

However, not all certification schemes carry equal weight. Critics argue that the proliferation of self-declared 'eco-friendly' labels has created significant consumer confusion. Without third-party verification, these labels amount to little more than marketing claims. The GSTC and similar bodies require on-site audits and documented evidence of reduced water consumption, renewable energy use, and responsible waste management before granting certification. Properties that meet these benchmarks consistently attract higher guest satisfaction scores and stronger repeat-booking rates.

For hotel operators, pursuing certification is increasingly seen as a competitive necessity rather than a voluntary gesture. Markets in Europe and parts of Southeast Asia have seen occupancy premiums of up to twelve percent for certified properties over non-certified competitors offering comparable amenities. As traveler expectations continue to evolve, the distinction between genuine sustainability credentials and superficial branding is becoming harder to obscure.
