Q1
The move-in date listed in the proposal is ＿＿, and the final schedule will be confirmed once the owner approves the lease amendment.
【中譯】提案中列出的搬入日期屬__，最終時間表將在業主批准租約修訂後確認。
A
tentative
B

C

D

解題關鍵
tentative = 暫定的（尚待最終確認），常見搭配：tentative date / tentative schedule / tentative agreement。句末 'will be confirmed once...' 明確暗示現在仍為待確認狀態，與 tentative 語義完全契合。
陷阱提示
temporary 表「持續時間短的/臨時性的」，側重時間長度，而 tentative 側重「尚未最終確定」；兩者語義有別，不可混用。

Q3
Only after the revised proposal ＿＿ by the board will the project receive full funding.
【中譯】只有在董事會批准修訂提案後，該專案才能獲得完整資金。
A
approves
B
approved
C
will approve
D
has been approved
解題關鍵
only after 引導的副詞子句中，主詞為 the revised proposal（提案），與動詞的關係為「被批准」，應用被動語態；子句本身不倒裝（倒裝在主句）；現在完成式 has been approved 表示「已被批准」，配合主句未來式 will receive，時態邏輯正確。
陷阱提示
only after 放句首時，主句須倒裝（will + S + V），但 only after 引導的子句本身不倒裝。另需辨別主詞 the revised proposal 與動詞的主被動關係。
文法補充
Only after + 子句（正常語序），主句倒裝：Only after she left did he realize the truth. 被動：Only after the report has been reviewed will the decision be made.
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
approvesv(三單現)/əˈpruːvz/批准（主動）主詞為 the board，接主動語態 approves，但 only after 倒裝結構要求助動詞提前，且語意應為提案被批准
approvedv(過去式)/əˈpruːvd/批准了（過去）時態與未來情境不符，且倒裝結構需助動詞+主詞
will approvev(未來式)/wɪl əˈpruːv/將會批准（主動）主動語態但語意應為提案被批准（被動），且倒裝結構在 only after 子句中不適用
has been approvedv(現完被動)/hæz bɪn əˈpruːvd/已獲批准（被動）現在完成式被動，表示提案已被批准，符合 only after 子句的語意與時態邏輯

單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
in thatconj.phr./ɪn ðæt/因為、在於（正式原因子句）✓ unique in that it... = 獨特之處在於…，正式書面英語連接詞

A growing number of companies engaged in business-to-business transactions are re-evaluating how they handle contractual disputes. Traditionally, litigation has been the default mechanism, but its high costs and lengthy timelines have prompted many firms to explore alternative dispute resolution methods. Arbitration and mediation, in particular, have gained traction as more efficient and confidential options.

According to a recent industry report published by the International Commerce Advisory Group, over 60 percent of surveyed corporations now include arbitration clauses in their standard vendor contracts. The report notes that arbitration decisions, while binding, are generally faster and less expensive than court proceedings. Mediation, though non-binding, is favored for preserving long-term business relationships, as it encourages parties to negotiate mutually acceptable solutions rather than imposing outcomes.

Despite the advantages, critics caution that alternative mechanisms are not without drawbacks. Arbitration panels, for instance, can be costly to assemble, and their decisions offer limited grounds for appeal. The report concludes with a cautious endorsement of hybrid approaches, recommending that companies tailor their dispute resolution strategies to the nature and scale of each contract rather than adopting a one-size-fits-all policy.

TOEIC · article
The Business Case for Cloud Backup: Moving Beyond Local Storage

For many small and medium-sized enterprises, data backup has historically been an afterthought — a task handled by periodic manual transfers to external hard drives or, in larger organizations, on-premises server backups. However, as cyberattacks, hardware failures, and natural disasters continue to expose the fragility of local storage solutions, cloud-based backup services have emerged as the standard of care for business continuity planning.

The core appeal of cloud backup lies in its combination of automation, scalability, and off-site redundancy. Unlike traditional backup methods, which require manual intervention and are vulnerable to the same physical risks as the primary data source, cloud services automatically replicate data across geographically distributed servers. This means that in the event of a localized disaster, data recovery can begin almost immediately, minimizing downtime.

Cost has historically been a barrier to adoption, particularly for smaller businesses. However, the economics of cloud storage have shifted dramatically. Subscription pricing models now allow organizations to pay only for the capacity they use, scaling up or down in line with actual business needs. For businesses that previously invested heavily in on-premises infrastructure, the total cost of ownership comparison increasingly favors cloud solutions.

Security remains a common concern. Businesses are often reluctant to place sensitive data in third-party hands, despite the fact that reputable cloud providers typically invest far more in security infrastructure than any individual business could afford. End-to-end encryption, multi-factor authentication, and continuous threat monitoring have become standard features of enterprise-grade cloud backup offerings.

Ultimately, the shift toward cloud backup is not merely a technological trend but a strategic imperative. Organizations that fail to establish reliable data protection frameworks risk not only operational disruption but also significant regulatory and reputational consequences in the event of a breach.
