Q2
So impressive ＿＿ the new robotic prototypes at the expo that attendees lined up for over an hour to see the live demonstrations.
【中譯】博覽會上新款機器人原型機__如此令人印象深刻，與會者排隊超過一小時只為觀看現場示範。
A
were
B
was
C
have been
D
being
解題關鍵
「So + adj./adv. + be + S + that + S + V」是形容詞/副詞前置的結果倒裝句型。主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數，過去式 be 動詞用 were；that 引導結果子句（lined up = 過去式）。
陷阱提示
was 是最常見陷阱——須確認主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數名詞，因此 be 動詞必須用 were 而非 was。此外，being 為分詞形式，無法充當倒裝句的主要謂語動詞。
文法補充
So + adj./adv. + be + S + that 結果倒裝：將形容詞/副詞提前至句首強調，be 動詞隨之倒裝至主詞前，形成「so... be + S... that...」結構。時態與數須與主詞一致。比較正常語序：The prototypes were so impressive that...
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
wereaux./wɜːr/（過去式複數 be 動詞，倒裝主謂）So + adj. + were + S(複數) + that ✓（the prototypes = 複數）
wasaux./wɒz/（過去式單數 be 動詞）主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數，不用單數 was
have beenaux./hæv bɪn/（現在完成式）語境為過去事件（lined up），現在完成式時態不符敘事語境
beingv-ing/ˈbiːɪŋ/（現在分詞）being 不能在 So + adj. 倒裝結構中作謂語動詞

Q8
By the time the project manager arrived at the office, the team ＿＿ all the preliminary reports.
【中譯】等到專案經理抵達辦公室時，團隊__已完成了所有初步報告。
A
submitted
B
was submitting
C
had submitted
D
have submitted
解題關鍵
「by the time + 過去式子句」表示「到……時為止」，主句須用過去完成式（had + p.p.）表示在參照時間點之前已完成的動作。
陷阱提示
submitted（過去簡單式）是最常見的誤選，因為外觀簡潔；但 by the time 結構要求清楚標示兩個過去動作的先後順序，必須用 had submitted 才能呈現「先完成報告，後抵達」的時序。
文法補充
by the time + S + V(past simple), S + had + p.p. ← 過去完成式公式；cf. by the time + S + V(present), S + will have + p.p. ← 未來完成式
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
submittedv. (past simple)/səbˈmɪtɪd/提交（過去簡單式）過去某時點的動作，無先後順序強調
was submittingv. (past continuous)/wəz səbˈmɪtɪŋ/正在提交（過去進行式）強調過去某時點正在進行，與 by the time 不搭
had submittedv. (past perfect)/hæd səbˈmɪtɪd/已提交（過去完成式）by the time + 過去式 → 主句用過去完成式 ✓
have submittedv. (present perfect)/hæv səbˈmɪtɪd/已提交（現在完成式）現在完成式不可搭配過去時間點

TOEIC · article
Recent amendments to national labor law have significantly altered the regulatory framework governing flexible working arrangements. The revised statute now requires employers with more than fifty employees to formally document any agreements related to flexible hours, remote work eligibility, and overtime compensation, a requirement that previously applied only to companies with over two hundred staff.

Human resources consultants report that many mid-sized companies are struggling with the administrative burden of compliance. A survey conducted by the Employment Standards Advisory Board found that over sixty percent of affected businesses had not yet established the necessary documentation systems as of the third quarter. Consultants attribute this gap partly to ambiguity in the revised law's language, particularly around the definition of 'core working hours.'

Proponents argue that the stricter requirements will ultimately benefit workers by ensuring greater transparency and legal certainty in employment arrangements. Critics, however, contend that the short implementation window — just eight months from enactment — places disproportionate pressure on smaller enterprises that lack dedicated compliance departments.

TOEIC · article
As electric vehicle adoption accelerates globally, city governments and private developers are racing to build the charging infrastructure needed to support the transition. Yet despite the surge in EV sales, public charging stations remain unevenly distributed, with dense urban centers often oversupplied while suburban and rural areas face significant coverage gaps.

The root cause of this disparity lies largely in economics. Charging stations in high-traffic commercial districts generate returns quickly, making them attractive to private investors. Those in lower-density areas, however, require substantial subsidies to break even, and municipal governments are increasingly reluctant to commit public funds without clearer usage data.

This reluctance, critics argue, threatens to entrench inequality in EV access, effectively pricing out lower-income drivers who depend on public charging rather than home installation.
