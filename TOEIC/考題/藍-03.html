Q1
The procurement team decided to ＿＿ costs by renegotiating contracts with three major suppliers before the fiscal year ended.
【中譯】採購團隊決定在財政年度結束前透過重新協商三家主要供應商的合約來__成本。
A
lower
B
slash
C
cut
D
shrink
解題關鍵
cut costs = 削減成本（最常見正式商業用語）；slash 語氣過重非正式；lower 可用但不如 cut 固定搭配自然；shrink 偏被動縮小
陷阱提示
lower 近義但 cut costs 是更固定的商業搭配；slash 語氣太強
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
lowerv/ˈloʊər/降低（動詞）lower costs ✓（近義；但常用於形容詞/副詞語境；作動詞「降低成本」可用）
slashv/slæʃ/大幅削減（動詞）slash costs（大砍，語意過強且偏非正式；不符商業正式通知語境）
cutv/kʌt/削減；降低（動詞）cut costs ✓（最常見正式商業用語；cut costs by renegotiating = 透過重談合約削減成本）
shrinkv/ʃrɪŋk/縮減；收縮（動詞）shrink costs（偏自然縮小，不用於「主動削減成本」的商業操作）

As environmental concerns move to the forefront of corporate strategy, multinational companies are under growing scrutiny from investors, regulators, and consumers alike. Increasingly, these organizations are expected not only to meet internal environmental targets but also to ensure that their suppliers adhere to comparable standards. This shift has given rise to a new discipline within procurement: sustainable supply chain management.

Leading companies in sectors ranging from consumer electronics to fast fashion have begun embedding rigorous environmental criteria into their supplier selection processes. Rather than treating sustainability as a standalone initiative, forward-thinking procurement teams now evaluate vendors on carbon emissions, water usage, waste management practices, and adherence to internationally recognized frameworks such as ISO 14001 or the UN Global Compact. Suppliers that fail to meet these benchmarks risk being dropped from approved vendor lists entirely.

The business case for such measures is compelling. Companies that align their sourcing decisions with environmental principles often find that cost savings and brand equity improvements translate into measurable financial gains over the medium to long term. Reduced energy consumption across the supply chain lowers operational costs, while demonstrated commitment to environmental stewardship attracts sustainability-conscious customers and institutional investors.

Of course, implementation is not without challenges. Smaller suppliers in emerging markets may lack the resources or technical expertise to comply with stringent environmental requirements imposed by their larger partners. To address this gap, several multinational firms have launched capacity-building programs, offering training, auditing support, and financing options to help smaller vendors meet the required thresholds. Such collaborative approaches reflect an understanding that sustainable supply chains are built through partnership, not simply through mandates.
醫療科技：基因療法的臨床突破與倫理監管挑戰
TOEIC · article
Recent clinical trials have demonstrated remarkable results for gene therapy treatments targeting rare hereditary disorders. Unlike conventional pharmaceutical interventions, which typically manage symptoms rather than address their root causes, gene therapy operates by introducing corrective genetic material directly into a patient's cells. For conditions caused by single-gene mutations — such as spinal muscular atrophy and certain forms of severe combined immunodeficiency — early trials have achieved outcomes previously considered unattainable, with some patients experiencing near-complete functional recovery.

Despite this scientific optimism, the path to widespread clinical adoption remains fraught with challenges. The high cost of gene therapy treatments — some exceeding one million dollars per patient — has raised profound questions about equitable access within both public and private healthcare systems. Regulatory agencies worldwide are also working to establish robust oversight mechanisms, as the long-term effects of many therapies remain incompletely understood.

Industry analysts suggest that gene therapy will most likely follow a trajectory similar to that of other transformative medical technologies: an initial period of restricted, high-cost availability followed by gradual cost reduction as manufacturing processes improve and competitive market dynamics take effect. Whether this timeline will align with the urgency felt by patients with life-threatening conditions, however, remains an open and ethically charged question.
