Q7
The finance team ＿＿ the quarterly report before the CEO arrived for the morning briefing.
【中譯】財務團隊在執行長來開早會之前，__ 季度報告。
A
has completed
B
had completed
C
completed
D
was completing
解題關鍵
句中有 before the CEO arrived（過去式），表示「抵達」是過去事件，而「完成報告」發生在此之前，因此需用過去完成式 had completed，表達「過去的過去」。
陷阱提示
has completed（現在完成式）用於與現在相關的情境，此句已明確為過去事件，不可用；completed（過去式）無法表達兩個過去動作的先後順序；was completing（過去進行式）暗示動作尚未完成，與 before 子句矛盾。
文法補充
過去完成式（Past Perfect）= had + p.p.，用於表示發生在另一過去動作或時間點之前已完成的動作，常與 before、by the time、when 等連接詞搭配。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
has completedv. phr./hæz kəmˈpliːtɪd/已完成（現在完成式）現在完成式，強調與現在的關聯
had completedv. phr./hæd kəmˈpliːtɪd/已完成（過去完成式）過去完成式，表示先於另一過去動作完成
completedv./kəmˈpliːtɪd/完成了（簡單過去式）簡單過去式，表示過去某時完成的動作
was completingv. phr./wəz kəmˈpliːtɪŋ/正在完成（過去進行式）過去進行式，表示過去某時正在進行的動作

法律合規：ESG 資訊揭露的跨境執法挑戰
TOEIC · report
The global push for greater corporate transparency has led to a rapid expansion of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) disclosure requirements across major economies. The European Union's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive, which took effect in 2024, now obligates thousands of companies to publish detailed sustainability data, covering areas from carbon emissions to labor practices. Similar frameworks are advancing in the United States and Asia.

However, the enforcement of these standards across borders remains deeply problematic. Multinational corporations frequently operate under conflicting regulatory regimes, where disclosure requirements in one jurisdiction may directly contradict those of another. Legal experts caution that companies navigating these overlapping obligations face not only compliance costs but also significant litigation risk, particularly when their subsidiaries are subject to local laws that restrict the release of certain data.

Analysts observing these developments maintain a cautiously skeptical outlook. While acknowledging that standardized global ESG reporting frameworks are theoretically desirable, they note that political resistance in key markets and the absence of an international enforcement body make meaningful convergence unlikely in the near term. For companies, the practical implication is an ongoing need for jurisdiction-specific legal counsel and adaptive compliance strategies.
