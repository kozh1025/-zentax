Q1
The investigation revealed that the data breach had been ＿＿ by a former employee who retained access credentials after leaving the company.
【中譯】調查顯示，該數據洩露事件是由一名離職後仍保有存取憑證的前員工__的。
A
perpetrated
B
penetrated
C
permeated
D
generated
解題關鍵
perpetrate 為正式法律/資安語彙，指「犯下（罪行、侵權行為、違規）」，常見搭配：perpetrate a breach / fraud / crime。
陷阱提示
penetrated 語意最接近（資安語境），但 penetrate 強調從外部突破（系統被攻破），而 perpetrate 強調主動犯行（人為犯罪）。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
perpetratedv. (p.p.)/ˈpɜːrpɪtreɪtɪd/犯下（罪行/違規）✓ perpetrate a breach / crime = 犯下（違規/洩露），正式法律/資安用語
penetratedv. (p.p.)/ˈpenɪtreɪtɪd/滲透、穿透語意相近但主詞角色不同：penetrate 指外部入侵系統（由攻擊方穿透），非「犯下」行為
permeatedv. (p.p.)/ˈpɜːrmieɪtɪd/滲透、瀰漫（液體/氣體）語意不符：permeate 指緩慢滲透（如氣味），不用於犯罪行為
generatedv. (p.p.)/ˈdʒenəreɪtɪd/產生、生成語意不符：generate 指創造或產生，非犯下罪行
字詞家族
perpetrate
犯下（perpetrate a fraud）
perpetrator
名詞：犯罪者、肇事者
perpetration
名詞：犯行
commit
B1 通俗同義替換：犯下（commit a crime）

Low-Code Platforms: Accelerating Enterprise Adoption and Its Limits

Low-code and no-code (LCNC) development platforms, which allow users to build software applications with minimal manual programming, have seen rapid enterprise adoption over the past three years. According to a 2025 Gartner forecast, over 65 percent of application development activity at large enterprises will be handled by non-specialist developers — so-called "citizen developers" — by 2027. Vendors such as Microsoft Power Apps, OutSystems, and Mendix have attracted billions in investment, buoyed by demand from organizations seeking to reduce IT backlogs and accelerate digital transformation.

Despite this momentum, LCNC platforms face meaningful constraints. Governance remains a persistent concern: provided that adequate oversight frameworks are not established, organizations risk creating a proliferation of poorly documented, shadow IT applications that bypass security and compliance standards. Furthermore, platform dependence poses long-term strategic risks, as companies that rely exclusively on a single vendor's ecosystem may face costly and disruptive migrations if pricing models change or the platform discontinues support for certain features.

Industry analysts largely agree that LCNC platforms are best suited as complements to, rather than replacements for, traditional development. The most successful implementations are those in which citizen developers work alongside professional software engineers, with the latter maintaining oversight of architecture, data governance, and integration standards. As the technology matures, organizations that treat LCNC adoption as a strategic capability — rather than a quick fix — are better positioned to extract sustained value.

Wearable Health Technology: Transforming Personal Wellness Monitoring

The market for wearable health monitoring devices has expanded dramatically over the past five years, driven by advances in sensor miniaturization, artificial intelligence, and consumer demand for proactive health management. Devices that once measured only step counts and heart rate can now track blood oxygen levels, detect irregular heart rhythms, monitor sleep cycles, and even estimate stress indicators through galvanic skin response.

By virtue of their continuous data collection capabilities, wearables offer clinicians insights that were previously impossible to obtain outside a hospital setting. Several studies have demonstrated that early detection of atrial fibrillation via wearable electrocardiogram sensors has reduced emergency cardiac admissions in trial populations by up to twenty-two percent.

Regulatory bodies in multiple jurisdictions are currently developing frameworks to classify wearable devices according to clinical risk levels. Manufacturers who wish to market their products with diagnostic claims must comply with stricter testing and certification standards.

Industry analysts expect the wearable health market to reach 180 billion dollars globally by 2030, with growth concentrated in chronic disease management, eldercare applications, and corporate wellness programs.
