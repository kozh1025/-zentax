Q1
So impressive ＿＿ the new robotic prototypes at the expo that attendees lined up for over an hour to see the live demonstrations.
【中譯】博覽會上新款機器人原型機__如此令人印象深刻，與會者排隊超過一小時只為觀看現場示範。
A
were
B
was
C
have been
D
being
解題關鍵
「So + adj./adv. + be + S + that + S + V」是形容詞/副詞前置的結果倒裝句型。主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數，過去式 be 動詞用 were；that 引導結果子句（lined up = 過去式）。
陷阱提示
was 是最常見陷阱——須確認主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數名詞，因此 be 動詞必須用 were 而非 was。此外，being 為分詞形式，無法充當倒裝句的主要謂語動詞。
文法補充
So + adj./adv. + be + S + that 結果倒裝：將形容詞/副詞提前至句首強調，be 動詞隨之倒裝至主詞前，形成「so... be + S... that...」結構。時態與數須與主詞一致。比較正常語序：The prototypes were so impressive that...
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
wereaux./wɜːr/（過去式複數 be 動詞，倒裝主謂）So + adj. + were + S(複數) + that ✓（the prototypes = 複數）
wasaux./wɒz/（過去式單數 be 動詞）主詞 the new robotic prototypes 為複數，不用單數 was
have beenaux./hæv bɪn/（現在完成式）語境為過去事件（lined up），現在完成式時態不符敘事語境
beingv-ing/ˈbiːɪŋ/（現在分詞）being 不能在 So + adj. 倒裝結構中作謂語動詞
Q2
＿＿ did the proposed changes reduce operating costs, but they also improved overall team morale.
【中譯】所提出的變更 __ 降低了營運成本，還改善了整體團隊士氣。
A

B

C

D

解題關鍵
否定副詞片語 Not only 置於句首時，主句必須倒裝（助動詞提前）：Not only + did + S + V...，but also 引導第二子句恢復正常語序。
陷阱提示
(C) Not only they 是最常見陷阱：學習者知道用 Not only 但忘記倒裝，保留了正常語序 they + did，導致語法錯誤。
文法補充
否定倒裝：Not only + aux./do + S + V..., but also... 類似結構：Neither/Nor did S V；Seldom/Rarely/Never + aux. + S + V
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
Not only否定副詞片語（倒裝首）置首觸發主句倒裝：Not only + did + S + VNot only...but also 標準倒裝結構 ✓
Only not錯誤語序非標準英語結構，語意與語序均錯誤✗ 不存在此結構
Not only they缺倒裝（保留正常語序）否定副詞置首但未倒裝，語法錯誤✗ 須倒裝：Not only did they
They not only無倒裝（主語前置）主語 They 在前，not only 置中，無倒裝效果✗ 非強調倒裝結構
Q3
＿＿ all the client feedback, the product team was able to finalize the improvements before the release date.
【中譯】在__所有客戶的意見回饋後，產品團隊得以在發布日期前完成改善工作。
A
Having reviewed
B
After reviewing
C
Reviewed
D
To review
解題關鍵
Having + pp 為完成式分詞構句，表示主要子句動作發生前已完成的動作，主詞與主句主詞相同（the product team）。本題強調「先審閱完，才能完成改善」，因此用完成式分詞 Having reviewed。
文法補充
完成式分詞構句（Perfect Participial Phrase）：Having + Vpp, S + V...。表示分詞動作在主要動作之前已完成，主詞須與主句相同。與 After + V-ing 意思相近，但 Having + pp 更強調時間先後關係，且為較正式的書面用法。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
Having reviewedparticiple phrase/ˈhævɪŋ rɪˈvjuːd/（完成式分詞構句）已審閱過Having reviewed all the client feedback, the product team was able to finalize the improvements before the release date. ✓
After reviewingconjunction phrase/ˈæftər rɪˈvjuːɪŋ/（介系詞片語）在審閱之後After reviewing the proposal, the manager approved the budget request.
Reviewedpast participle/rɪˈvjuːd/（過去分詞，被動語態起首）被審閱的Reviewed by the committee, the report was finally approved.
To reviewinfinitive/tuː rɪˈvjuː/（不定詞）為了審閱To review the contract thoroughly, she stayed late at the office.

Q11
The board approved the merger with a rival firm ＿＿ financial performance had been declining for two consecutive quarters.
【中譯】董事會批准了與一家競爭公司的合併案，該公司的財務績效已連續兩季下滑。
A

B

C

D

解題關鍵
空格後接 financial performance（名詞片語），說明空格需要所有格關係代名詞。先行詞 a rival firm 是組織（非人），但 whose 可用於代替組織的所有格（firm whose financial performance）。which 的所有格必須寫成 of which the financial performance 或 the financial performance of which；what 無先行詞；that 無所有格形式。
陷阱提示
學習者常誤認 whose 只能代替「人」，但英文中 whose 可合法用於代替組織、公司、國家等非人稱先行詞，這是高頻考點。
文法補充
關係代名詞所有格規則：whose 是唯一的關係代名詞所有格形式，可用於先行詞為人、組織、動物或事物（如 a country whose economy, a company whose revenue）。which 本身無所有格，需改用 of which。that 無所有格形式。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法

whichrel. pron./wɪtʃ/（關係代名詞，代替事物）which 不能單獨作所有格（需用 of which）

醫療科技：基因療法的臨床突破與倫理監管挑戰
TOEIC · article
Recent clinical trials have demonstrated remarkable results for gene therapy treatments targeting rare hereditary disorders. Unlike conventional pharmaceutical interventions, which typically manage symptoms rather than address their root causes, gene therapy operates by introducing corrective genetic material directly into a patient's cells. For conditions caused by single-gene mutations — such as spinal muscular atrophy and certain forms of severe combined immunodeficiency — early trials have achieved outcomes previously considered unattainable, with some patients experiencing near-complete functional recovery.

Despite this scientific optimism, the path to widespread clinical adoption remains fraught with challenges. The high cost of gene therapy treatments — some exceeding one million dollars per patient — has raised profound questions about equitable access within both public and private healthcare systems. Regulatory agencies worldwide are also working to establish robust oversight mechanisms, as the long-term effects of many therapies remain incompletely understood.

Industry analysts suggest that gene therapy will most likely follow a trajectory similar to that of other transformative medical technologies: an initial period of restricted, high-cost availability followed by gradual cost reduction as manufacturing processes improve and competitive market dynamics take effect. Whether this timeline will align with the urgency felt by patients with life-threatening conditions, however, remains an open and ethically charged question.

Mobile Payments and Financial Inclusion: A Transformative Opportunity

The global adoption of mobile payment systems has accelerated dramatically over the past five years, driven by smartphone penetration, improved internet connectivity, and the post-pandemic shift toward contactless transactions. Markets in Southeast Asia and sub-Saharan Africa have seen particularly rapid growth, with mobile money platforms enabling millions of previously unbanked individuals to access basic financial services for the first time.

Proponents of mobile payments argue that these technologies can serve as a bridge to broader financial inclusion. Unlike traditional banking, which often requires physical branch visits, minimum account balances, and extensive documentation, mobile payment platforms typically offer low barriers to entry. A user in a rural area can transfer funds, pay utility bills, and even access microloans using only a basic smartphone and a registered SIM card.

However, critics caution that not all users benefit equally. Transaction fees, while small individually, can accumulate into a significant burden for low-income households that conduct frequent small-value transfers. Furthermore, digital literacy gaps and limited access to reliable electricity in some regions continue to exclude the most vulnerable populations from fully participating in the digital economy.

Regulators and industry stakeholders are increasingly aware of these tensions. Several governments have introduced fee caps on peer-to-peer transfers and mandated interoperability between competing platforms, allowing consumers to transact across different mobile money providers without additional charges. Whether these measures will be sufficient to ensure equitable access remains an open question, but the momentum behind mobile payment adoption shows no sign of slowing.
