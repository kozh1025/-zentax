Q1
Only after the revised proposal ＿＿ by the board will the project receive full funding.
【中譯】只有在董事會批准修訂提案後，該專案才能獲得完整資金。
A
approves
B
approved
C
will approve
D
has been approved
解題關鍵
only after 引導的副詞子句中，主詞為 the revised proposal（提案），與動詞的關係為「被批准」，應用被動語態；子句本身不倒裝（倒裝在主句）；現在完成式 has been approved 表示「已被批准」，配合主句未來式 will receive，時態邏輯正確。
陷阱提示
only after 放句首時，主句須倒裝（will + S + V），但 only after 引導的子句本身不倒裝。另需辨別主詞 the revised proposal 與動詞的主被動關係。
文法補充
Only after + 子句（正常語序），主句倒裝：Only after she left did he realize the truth. 被動：Only after the report has been reviewed will the decision be made.
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
approvesv(三單現)/əˈpruːvz/批准（主動）主詞為 the board，接主動語態 approves，但 only after 倒裝結構要求助動詞提前，且語意應為提案被批准
approvedv(過去式)/əˈpruːvd/批准了（過去）時態與未來情境不符，且倒裝結構需助動詞+主詞
will approvev(未來式)/wɪl əˈpruːv/將會批准（主動）主動語態但語意應為提案被批准（被動），且倒裝結構在 only after 子句中不適用
has been approvedv(現完被動)/hæz bɪn əˈpruːvd/已獲批准（被動）現在完成式被動，表示提案已被批准，符合 only after 子句的語意與時態邏輯

Q3
The supervisor advised team members to discuss project concerns directly with their manager ＿＿ sending impersonal messages through the company's ticketing system.
【中譯】主管建議團隊成員直接與經理討論專案疑慮，__透過公司工單系統發送缺乏人情味的訊息。
A

B

C

D

解題關鍵
rather than + V-ing 表選擇偏好的對比（discuss...rather than sending），強調主動選擇 A 優先於 B；介系詞後接動名詞形式
陷阱提示

文法補充
rather than 作介系詞時後接 V-ing（平行動名詞）；作對等連接詞時後接 V 原形（discuss...rather than send...）；兩種形式均可，但 + V-ing 更常見於正式書面
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法

The vendor agreement will remain valid ＿＿ all deliveries are made within the agreed timeframes.

B

C

解題關鍵
so long as 與 as long as 同義，均表「只要……就」的條件；provided that 亦可表條件但語氣偏向法律合約前提。此題語境為「持續有效的條件」，so long as 最為自然，且為本輪目標文法。in case 表「以防萬一」，語意不符。

文法補充
條件副詞子句連接詞：so long as = as long as（持續條件）vs provided that（法律前提條件）

Q10
The sales figures ＿＿ were presented at yesterday's meeting showed a significant increase in overseas markets.
【中譯】昨天會議上所呈現的銷售數據顯示海外市場有顯著成長。
A
who
B
that
C
whose
D
whom
解題關鍵
先行詞 The sales figures 是事物，需用 that 或 which；子句中 were presented 缺主詞，故填關係代名詞作主格；that 在限定性關係子句中最常見，為正確答案。
陷阱提示
who 與 whom 皆只用於人，whose 表所有格，三者皆不符；that 與 which 均可用於事物，但 that 在限定性子句中更為普遍。
文法補充
限定性關係子句（restrictive clause）修飾事物時，主格用 that 或 which；受格可省略或用 that/which。
單字/片語詞性音標主要意思搭配用法
whopron/huː/（人）關係代名詞先行詞必須是人，sales figures 為事物，不適用
thatpron/ðæt/（人或物）關係代名詞that 可修飾人或事物，且可在限定性子句中取代 which ✓
whosepron/huːz/（所有格）關係代名詞whose 表所有關係，後接名詞，此處無所有格語意
whompron/huːm/（人）受格關係代名詞whom 修飾人，且為受格形式，不適用於事物主詞

教育/出版：學術期刊數位訂閱分析
TOEIC · article
Digital Subscriptions Reshape Academic Publishing Landscape

Universities around the world are rapidly transitioning from print journal subscriptions to digital access models, driven by cost pressures, space limitations, and growing demand for remote access. In view of these shifts, academic publishers have been compelled to redesign their pricing structures and content delivery systems.

A comprehensive study published by the Consortium of Research Libraries surveyed 450 institutions across 28 countries and found that digital journal subscriptions now account for over 78 percent of total academic publication spending. The report also highlighted significant disparities in subscription costs between large research universities and smaller liberal arts colleges, with the latter often facing disproportionately higher per-article fees.

Critics have long raised concerns about the dominance of a handful of major publishers who control the pricing of thousands of journals. Several universities have responded by withdrawing from expensive bundle agreements and investing in open-access publishing initiatives instead. Proponents argue that open-access models democratize knowledge by making research freely available to institutions that cannot afford premium subscriptions.

Despite the growing momentum of open access, commercial publishers maintain that subscription revenues are necessary to fund peer-review processes and maintain editorial quality. The debate over how to balance financial sustainability with broad knowledge accessibility continues to shape academic publishing policy worldwide.

AgriTech Asia 2027: How Technology Is Feeding the Future

The AgriTech Asia 2027 Exposition, held last week at the Singapore International Convention Centre, drew over forty thousand visitors and featured more than three hundred exhibitors from twenty-six countries. The event, widely regarded as the region's most important platform for agricultural innovation, showcased a range of technologies aimed at addressing pressing challenges in food production, supply chain efficiency, and environmental sustainability.

Among the most discussed innovations was precision irrigation technology, which uses satellite data and soil sensors to optimize water usage on a field-by-field basis. Several companies demonstrated systems capable of reducing water consumption by up to forty percent compared to conventional irrigation methods, a development of particular significance given the increasing frequency of drought conditions across Southeast and South Asia.

Another major theme at the exposition was the integration of AI-driven pest detection tools. On the verge of widespread commercial deployment, these systems use image recognition algorithms trained on millions of crop photographs to identify infestations within minutes — dramatically reducing the need for broad-spectrum pesticide applications.

While enthusiasm for these technologies was high, several agricultural economists attending the event cautioned that adoption rates among smallholder farmers remain stubbornly low due to the high upfront costs of hardware and the limited availability of technical training in rural areas. Bridging this gap, they argued, will require coordinated investment from both the private sector and governments across the region.
